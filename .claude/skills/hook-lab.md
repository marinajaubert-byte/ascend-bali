# Hook Lab

Generate a menu of opening lines for Marina's content — tested across five psychological mechanisms, specific to her topic and audience.

## How to run
Ask Marina: "What's the topic or post you need a hook for?"

Then generate 5 hooks using different mechanisms:

**1. The contradiction hook** — opens with something that shouldn't be true but is
Format: "Most people [X]. But [unexpected truth]."
Marina example: "Most people fly to Bali for sun. The founders who change their business come for something else entirely."

**2. The pain hook** — names the frustration before offering the release
Format: "[Specific feeling] is [specific situation]."
Marina example: "Leaving a networking event feeling more alone than when you arrived — that's not you. That's the event."

**3. The story hook** — drops into a scene mid-action
Format: Start in a specific moment, no setup.
Marina example: "He had been trying to get into that room for two years. We got him there in 48 hours."

**4. The curiosity hook** — creates an information gap the reader has to close
Format: Reveal a partial truth that demands completion.
Marina example: "There's a room in Bali that most business visitors never find. Not because it's hidden. Because they're asking the wrong people."

**5. The bold claim hook** — a direct, confident statement that makes the reader react
Format: State the uncomfortable or contrarian truth flat.
Marina example: "Networking events are designed for the organiser, not for you."

## Output
All 5 hooks written for Marina's specific topic.
**Best pick:** Recommend one with a one-line reason.
**Worst pick:** Flag the weakest and explain why it won't land.
