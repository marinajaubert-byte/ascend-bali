# Substack

Write a Substack piece in Marina's voice — not a blog post, not a listicle. A real piece of writing that earns the read and leaves something behind.

## How to run
Ask Marina:
1. "What's the angle — do you have a specific topic, or do you want me to suggest one based on what's current?"
2. "Is this for existing subscribers (warm) or for new reach (cold)?"

If no angle is given, use the Last 30 output to suggest 3 options and let Marina choose.

## Structure
A Marina Substack piece follows this arc:
1. **Open with a contradiction or provocation** — something that creates tension in the first two sentences
2. **The real thing** — what the post is actually about, arrived at from an angle that surprises
3. **The argument** — Marina's perspective, backed by observation or experience (not data or credentials)
4. **The turn** — the moment where the piece shifts from problem to possibility
5. **The landing** — not a call to action. A thought to carry away. Something that lingers.

## Rules
- Target 400–700 words. Long enough to be substantive, short enough to be read in one sitting.
- No subheadings — this is a piece of writing, not a guide
- No bullet points
- Open with the strongest possible first sentence — Marina's voice is made or broken in line one
- The piece should reveal something Marina actually believes, not something generically useful

## Substack title
Generate 3 title options — one direct, one intriguing, one that names the tension.
