# Client Comms

Write any communication Marina needs to send to a current client — pre-visit prep, during-visit updates, introduction messages, or post-visit follow-through.

## How to run
Ask Marina:
1. "Which client is this for and where are they in the journey?"
2. "What specifically needs to be communicated?"
3. "What tone is appropriate — warm and casual, or more formal?"

## Client communication stages

**Pre-visit (1–2 weeks before arrival):**
The "everything is in motion" message. Marina confirms she has the introductions lined up, sets expectations for how the visit will flow, and makes the client feel they are in the best possible hands.

**Day of arrival:**
Short, warm. Welcome to Bali. A reminder of what's planned and Marina's contact details for the visit.

**Introduction message:**
When Marina introduces two people from her network — this message needs to be warm, specific, and make both people feel the introduction was made with intention. Not a generic "I'd like to introduce X and Y."

**During-visit check-in:**
A quick message to make sure things are going well and address any needs that have arisen.

**Post-visit:**
Genuine follow-up. Not a CTA to rebook — an authentic check-in on how the introductions are developing. This is where referrals are born.

## Output format
**Message for [Client Name] — [Stage]:**
Full draft ready to send. Tone calibrated to the client relationship.
