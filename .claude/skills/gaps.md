# Gaps

Find the white space — what Marina's market is asking for that no one is currently offering well. Turn market gaps into positioning opportunities.

## How to run
Combine insights from Last 30, Competitor Intel, and Audience research. Then look for gaps across three levels:

**Offer gaps:** What are Marina's clients asking for that isn't currently available — or not packaged well?
Examples: A specific type of introduction (e.g. family office investors), a format (e.g. a curated retreat vs. a one-off dinner), a geography (e.g. a Bali-Singapore bridge service).

**Content gaps:** What questions are being asked in Marina's space that nobody is answering clearly and honestly?
Examples: What does it actually cost to make a business introduction in Bali? What should you do in Bali if you have 48 hours and need to find a tech partner?

**Distribution gaps:** Where is Marina's audience that no one is reaching them?
Examples: Are founder communities in Singapore talking about Bali but no Bali-based connector has a presence there?

## Output format

**Gap Report**

**3 offer gaps** — each with: the gap, evidence it exists, rough idea of how Marina could fill it, effort required.

**3 content gaps** — each with: the question no one is answering, the angle Marina could own, the platform where this would land.

**The biggest opportunity:**
One paragraph on the single most compelling gap — and what Marina would need to do to be the person who fills it.
