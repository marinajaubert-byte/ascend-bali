# Launch Manager

Build and manage Marina's launch sequence — from pre-launch to open to close. A launch for Ascend Global is not a megaphone moment; it is a carefully curated invitation to the right people.

## How to run
Ask Marina:
1. "What are we launching — a new offer, an event, a dinner, a new community tier?"
2. "What is the launch window — when does it open and close?"
3. "Who specifically are we launching to — existing audience, warm leads, a cold list, or all three?"

## Launch sequence (default 10-day)

**Days 1–3 — The build:**
Content that primes the audience without announcing anything. The topic. The problem. The philosophy. Make people feel the need before they know there's an offer.

**Day 4 — The open:**
The announcement. Clear, specific, in Marina's voice. What it is, who it's for, what it costs, how to get it. One link. One action.

**Days 5–7 — The proof and the push:**
Case study, testimonial, or specific story of a result Marina has created. Behind-the-scenes of what the offer looks like in practice. Answer the objection before it's raised.

**Day 8 — The urgency:**
Not artificial. A genuine reason — limited spaces, a deadline, a window that closes. Marina only works with a small number of clients; that is the natural scarcity.

**Days 9–10 — The close:**
Final invitation. Warm, direct, no pressure. "This is the last time I'll mention this" — and mean it.

## Output format

**Launch Plan — [Offer/Event Name]**
Day-by-day content and communication plan.
Platform by platform.
The close date and what happens after it.
