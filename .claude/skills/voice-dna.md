# Voice DNA

Extract and document Marina's writing voice from samples she provides, producing a reusable Voice DNA profile that every other skill references when writing content for her.

## How to run
Ask Marina to paste 3–5 pieces of her own writing — captions, DMs, emails, voice note transcripts, anything she actually wrote or said. Then analyse them across these dimensions:

**Sentence rhythm** — How long are her sentences? Does she use short punches followed by longer thoughts? Where does she breathe?
**Opening patterns** — How does she start a piece? With a statement, a question, a contrast?
**Closing patterns** — How does she land? Call to action, reflection, open question?
**Vocabulary fingerprint** — Which words appear repeatedly? Which words would she never use?
**Structural moves** — Does she use lists? Contrasts? Rhetorical questions? Stories?
**Emotional register** — Where does warmth show up? Where does conviction show up?
**What she never does** — AI tells, corporate language, hollow phrases to ban

## Output format
Produce a structured Voice DNA card with:
1. **The voice in one sentence** — a single line that captures the essence
2. **Rhythm rules** — 3–5 sentence structure rules
3. **Vocabulary bank** — 15 words/phrases she owns; 10 words/phrases banned
4. **Opening moves** — 3 patterns she uses to start content
5. **Closing moves** — 3 patterns she uses to land content
6. **The test** — one question to ask of any draft: "Does this sound like Marina or like a template?"

## Marina's known voice markers (seed)
- Uses contrasts: fear vs. freedom, transactional vs. real, surface vs. depth
- Inclusive "we" and "our" — never preachy
- Big philosophical ideas grounded in practical reality
- Short declarative → longer expansive rhythm
- Words she owns: connection, freedom, depth, meaning, purpose, room, introduction, real, genuine, giving
- Words to ban: "game-changing", "amazing", "hustle", "leverage", "synergy", hollow superlatives

Save this profile as the voice reference for all content skills.
