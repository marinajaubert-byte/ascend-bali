# Niche

Help Marina get ruthlessly clear on exactly who she serves, what specific problem she solves, and why she is the only person who can solve it the way she does.

## How to run
Ask Marina three questions before generating anything:
1. Who was your best client in the last 12 months — what made them the best?
2. Who do you wish you could work with more of?
3. Who have you turned away, or wished you had turned away?

Then map out:

**The niche matrix:**
- **Who:** Specific type of person (not "entrepreneurs" — what kind? From where? At what stage?)
- **When:** The specific moment or trigger that brings them to Marina (visiting Bali, expanding to SEA, raising their first round in Asia?)
- **What:** The exact transformation Marina provides (not "introductions" — what does the introduction unlock for them?)
- **Why Marina:** What lived experience, network, or access does Marina have that a competitor cannot replicate?

## Output format
**Niche Statement (working version):**
"Marina serves [specific who] who are [specific situation/trigger] and need [specific outcome] — and she is the only person who can do this because [specific proof of uniqueness]."

Then: Three ways to sharpen the niche further, with the trade-off of each.

The goal is not to narrow for narrowing's sake — it is to be the obvious choice for a specific person in a specific moment.
