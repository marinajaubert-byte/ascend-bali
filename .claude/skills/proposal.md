# Proposal

Write a bespoke proposal for a specific client — not a template, a genuine plan for their specific situation and what Marina will do to make their Bali visit count.

## How to run
Ask Marina:
1. "What did the client tell you they need?"
2. "What specific outcomes are you committing to?"
3. "What is the investment?"

## Proposal structure

**Opening:** Address the person, not a generic "Dear Client." Start by reflecting their situation back — what they shared, what they're trying to achieve, what this trip means for their business.

**The plan:** What Marina will specifically do for this client.
- The introductions she is confident she can make (specific types, not guarantees)
- Any events or dinners she'll include them in
- Support before, during, and after the visit
- The community access they'll receive

**Why Marina:** One paragraph. Not a bio. A statement of why Marina is uniquely positioned to deliver what this specific client needs. Her network, her knowledge of Bali, her philosophy.

**The investment:** Clear and direct. What it costs, what is included, what payment process looks like.

**The next step:** How to confirm. One action, not a menu of options.

## Output format
Full proposal, 300–500 words, in Marina's voice. Professional but warm — this should not feel like a contract. It should feel like the beginning of a curated experience.
