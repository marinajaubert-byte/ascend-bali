# Sales Call

Prepare Marina for a sales or discovery call — so she walks in knowing exactly what to uncover, what to offer, and what outcome to reach by the end of the conversation.

## How to run
Ask Marina:
1. "Who is the call with and what do we know about them so far?"
2. "What did they share in their intake form or prior message?"
3. "What does Marina want to achieve by the end of this call?"

## Call structure (for Marina)

**Opening (2 min):** Warm, not formal. Acknowledge where they are — "you're visiting Bali in [month], you mentioned you're looking to open doors in [market]." Show you read what they sent.

**Discovery (10–15 min):** Ask the questions that reveal whether Marina can genuinely help, and what "help" means to this specific person.
Key questions to uncover:
- What specifically do they need to make this trip worth it?
- What have they already tried — who have they already met in Bali?
- What does the right introduction actually unlock for them?
- How are they currently making connections when they visit new markets?
- What would make them say "that was the best business trip I've ever taken"?

**Positioning (5 min):** After listening, reflect back what you heard and position Ascend Global's offer to match. Not a pitch — a fit check. "Based on what you've described, here's what I think makes sense..."

**The close (3 min):** Not pressure. Clarity. What is the next step, and what happens after that?

## Output format
**Call Prep Brief for [Name]:**
- What we know about them
- 5 tailored discovery questions
- The 1 thing Marina must uncover on this call
- Positioning language for this specific client
- The close: proposed next step
