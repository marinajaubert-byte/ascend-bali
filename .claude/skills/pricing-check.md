# Pricing Check

Review Marina's current pricing against the value she delivers, the market she serves, and the positioning she wants to hold.

## How to run
Ask Marina:
1. "What are the current prices for each offer?"
2. "Are you consistently getting yes without hesitation, or are you getting a lot of price objections?"
3. "What is one introduction worth to a typical client — have you ever seen someone close a deal or raise money as a result of Marina's network?"

## Pricing analysis

**Value anchor:** What is the economic value of one successful introduction? A single enterprise client ($50K+ annual retainer), one investor meeting that leads to funding ($500K+), one partnership that opens a market — the ROI of Marina's work is enormous. Her pricing should be anchored to this, not to her time.

**Market position:** Premium positioning requires premium pricing. Underpricing a curated, high-access service signals that it isn't what it claims to be. Marina's ideal client will trust a $5,000 engagement more than a $500 one.

**Objection test:** If clients are consistently pushing back on price, the problem is usually not the price — it's that the value hasn't been communicated clearly enough.

**Competitor comparison:** What do comparable concierge and introduction services charge? How does Marina compare?

## Output format

**Pricing Review:**
- Current pricing assessment (honest)
- Recommended pricing for each offer with rationale
- The value anchor statement Marina should use in sales conversations
- Any price increase Marina should consider and how to communicate it to existing clients
