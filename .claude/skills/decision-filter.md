# Decision Filter

Help Marina make a clear, confident decision — by surfacing what she actually values, what the real trade-offs are, and what she'll likely think of this decision in 12 months.

## How to run
Ask Marina: "Tell me the decision and the options as you see them right now."

## Decision framework

**Clarify the decision:** Is Marina deciding between two options, or is there a third option (including "do nothing" or "decide later") that hasn't been named?

**The values test:** What does each option require Marina to prioritise — revenue, time, relationships, brand, freedom? Which of these is Marina least willing to compromise right now?

**The regret test (Bezos):** 10 years from now, which choice is Marina more likely to regret — taking the option, or not taking it?

**The energy test:** When Marina imagines herself 30 days into each option — which one feels like expansion, and which one feels like drag?

**The reversibility test:** Is this decision reversible? If yes, the cost of a "wrong" choice is much lower than it feels. If no, more care is warranted.

**The advisor test:** What would Marina's most trusted, most direct advisor tell her to do — and why?

## Output format

**Decision: [What Marina is deciding]**

Options clearly named.
Each filter applied with a short answer.
**Marina's decision (supported):** Not a recommendation — a reflection of what the filters reveal. Marina decides. The filter makes it clear.
**One condition:** If Marina proceeds with Option X, the one thing she must do to make it work.
