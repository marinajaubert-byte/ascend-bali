# Collab Pitch

Write a collaboration pitch for Marina — specific to the person or brand she wants to approach, clear on the value exchange, and written in Marina's voice.

## How to run
Ask Marina:
1. "Who is this pitch to and what do we know about them?"
2. "What kind of collaboration are we proposing?"
3. "Have you met this person before or is this a cold approach?"

## Pitch principles
- Open with something specific to them — their work, a piece of content, a mutual contact. Never generic.
- State the value for THEM before the value for Marina.
- Make the ask specific and easy to respond to — one clear next step, not a vague "let me know if you're interested."
- Keep it short. Long pitches signal that the sender values their own words more than the reader's time.
- Marina's tone: confident, genuine, peer-to-peer. Not fan-letter energy. Not corporate proposal energy.

## Output format

**Subject line** (for email) or **Opening line** (for DM)

**Full pitch** — 100–150 words maximum for first contact.

**Follow-up version** — if no response after 5–7 days, a shorter second touch.

**The ask:** Explicitly stated at the end — a call, a reply, a specific meeting.

Note: If the collaboration would require giving something significant away for free, flag it to Marina before finalising the pitch. Every collaboration should have clear value on both sides.
