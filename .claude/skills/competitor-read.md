# Competitor Read

Do a thorough read of one specific competitor — their positioning, content, offers, strengths, and gaps. Give Marina the intelligence she needs to sharpen her own edge.

## How to run
Ask Marina: "Who are we reading?" Then ask for their website, social handles, or any content Marina has seen from them.

Analyse across:

**Positioning:** How do they describe what they do? Who do they say they serve? What claim do they make?
**Offer:** What do they sell? How is it packaged? What does it cost (if visible)?
**Content:** What do they post? What topics? What tone? What gets the most engagement?
**Audience signals:** Who follows and engages? What does the comment section reveal about their clients?
**Strengths:** What are they genuinely doing well? Be honest — denial is not intelligence.
**Blind spots:** Where are they weak? What are they not offering? What complaints appear in their content or reviews?
**Threat level:** Are they a real competitive threat to Marina, or operating in a different lane?

## Output format

**Competitor Read: [Name]**

Section-by-section analysis as above.

**The one thing Marina should take from this:** A single insight — positioning shift, content angle, offer gap, or distribution move — that this competitor's existence reveals.

**The one thing Marina should never do:** What about this competitor's approach would be wrong for Ascend Global's brand?
