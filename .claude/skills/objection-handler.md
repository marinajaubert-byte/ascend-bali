# Objection Handler

Handle the specific objection a lead has raised — with honesty, not pressure. Marina's response to an objection should build trust, not close a sale at any cost.

## How to run
Ask Marina: "What did they say exactly?" The exact words matter.

## Common objections and how Marina handles them

**"I can make my own connections in Bali."**
Acknowledge it — many people can. Marina's value is not connections per se, it is the *right* connections, curated for the specific thing this person is trying to achieve. The people who benefit most from Marina are those who have tried the DIY version and found that Bali's real rooms are not accessible through a Google search or a co-working space event.

**"I'm not sure it's worth the investment."**
Return to value anchoring: what is one serious investor introduction worth? One partnership that opens a market? One client that stays for three years? Marina doesn't compete on price — she competes on ROI. If the lead cannot see the ROI, the fit may not be right.

**"I don't have much time in Bali."**
Limited time is exactly why Marina exists. Anyone can fill 5 days with random meetings. Marina fills 2 days with the right ones.

**"I've been to Bali before and didn't find much business value."**
This is Marina's strongest positioning moment. They've already run the experiment without Marina. Here is what changes with her.

**"Can I see some testimonials or proof?"**
A genuine and fair question. Provide the strongest evidence Marina has.

## Output format
**Response to: [specific objection]**
Full draft Marina can send or say verbatim. Then one-line note on whether this objection reveals a real fit issue or just needs better information.
