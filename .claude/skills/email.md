# Email

Write an email for Marina — to a lead, a client, a potential collaborator, or her list. Direct, warm, and purposeful. No padding.

## How to run
Ask Marina:
1. "Who is this email to and what's the relationship?" (new lead, existing client, cold outreach, warm follow-up)
2. "What is the one thing this email needs to accomplish?"
3. "Is there anything specific that happened before this email that I should reference?"

## Email principles
- **Subject line:** Specific enough to earn the open. Not clickbait. Not vague.
- **Opening:** Reference the context — never start cold with "I hope this finds you well."
- **Body:** One clear purpose. Say it once, directly. Expand only if it adds value.
- **Close:** One clear next step. Never two options. Make it easy to say yes.
- **Sign-off:** Marina — never over-formal, never over-casual.

## Output format
Produce:
- **Subject line** (and one alternative)
- **Full email body** (ready to send)
- **One-line note** on tone — e.g. "This is deliberately brief because X is a busy person. Expand if you want to establish more warmth first."

## Email types Marina uses
- **Lead reply** — responding to a new inquiry from the intake form
- **Follow-up** — after a first conversation, before a decision
- **Welcome** — first email to a confirmed client
- **Check-in** — during a client's Bali stay
- **Post-trip** — after a client's visit, relationship maintenance
- **Introduction email** — making the actual introduction between two people in Marina's network
