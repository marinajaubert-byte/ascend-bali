# Offer Architect

Design a compelling, clearly positioned offer for Marina — one that is easy to say yes to, priced correctly for the value delivered, and structured to match how her best clients want to buy.

## How to run
Ask Marina:
1. "What outcome does this offer deliver for the client?"
2. "Who specifically is this offer for?"
3. "What is the client's situation at the start, and what does it look like after?"
4. "What has Marina seen clients pay for in this space before?"

## Offer design framework

**The core transformation:** State the before and after in one sentence. The offer exists to move someone from Point A to Point B — be specific about both points.

**The vehicle:** How does the transformation happen? What does Marina actually do? (Introductions, dinners, concierge support, calls with Marina) — package it so it's clear and felt, not just listed.

**The delivery timeline:** When and how does the client receive value? Is this a one-trip engagement? An ongoing relationship? A defined package?

**The proof:** What results have happened before? Name specific outcomes (with permission or anonymised).

**The price:** Is the price anchored to the value (what is one right introduction worth?) or to time/effort? Premium services are priced on value, not hours.

**The name:** Name the offer something that signals the transformation, not the process. Not "Bali Introduction Package" — something that captures what it actually does.

## Output format
**Offer Blueprint:**
- Offer name
- One-sentence positioning
- What's included (3–5 elements)
- What's not included (manages expectations)
- Price and payment structure
- The guarantee or risk reversal (if applicable)
- Sales page intro paragraph (in Marina's voice)
